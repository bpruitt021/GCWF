Living with Grace

Receive it. Extend it. Live it. — A devotional on the gospel of grace.
God's Cloud of Witnesses
October 27, 2025 · Devotional · 4 min read

We live in a world that moves fast, judges quickly, and forgives slowly. Mistakes become labels, flaws become headlines, and people are often remembered more for their failures than for their potential. But the gospel paints a different picture—one filled with grace.

What is Grace?

Grace is the unearned, undeserved favor of God. We didn’t work for it. We didn’t qualify for it. We couldn’t earn it if we tried. Grace says: You are loved—even on your worst day. You are forgiven—even when you fall short. You are accepted—not because of what you’ve done, but because of what Christ has done.

"My grace is sufficient for you, for My power is made perfect in weakness." — 2 Corinthians 12:9

Grace for You: Receive It

Many believers accept God’s forgiveness in theory but struggle to live like they are truly forgiven. We replay old failures, rehearse old wounds, and carry guilt long after God has removed it. To live with grace is to wake up each day and say, "I am not who I was. I am who God says I am."

Grace for Others: Extend It

Grace is not just something we receive—it is something we give. Ephesians 4:32 says, "Be kind to one another, tenderhearted, forgiving one another, as God in Christ forgave you." Grace looks like choosing kindness over criticism, listening before judging, and forgiving when it’s easier to hold a grudge.

Grace in Daily Living

Grace is not a Sunday concept—it’s a daily lifestyle. When someone disappoints you — respond with patience. When you’re tempted to boast — choose humility. When you see someone hurting — offer compassion. When you stumble — get back up and thank God for mercy.

The Freedom of Grace

Grace frees us from the pressure to perform, pretend, or be perfect. It gives us room to grow. When we live with grace, we walk in freedom—and we help others do the same.

Final Encouragement

Living with grace means remembering how deeply God loves you and letting that love flow through you to others. It is not always easy, but it is always worth it. Today, choose grace: grace for yourself, grace for others, grace in every moment.
