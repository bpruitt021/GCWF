Author: Brandi Pruitt

Date: October 26, 2025

Hebrews 11 is often called "Hall of Faith." Like a hallway filled with portraits of great men and women, this chapter shows us believers who trusted God against all odds. None of them were perfect, but all of them had one thing in common: they walked by faith. Today, God is still calling His people to walk by faith- not by feelings, not by fear, not by what we can see, touch, or perdict- but by trusting in Him.

WALK BY FAITH — HEBREWS 11 Faith. We talk about it, we sing about it, and we quote Scriptures about it. But what does it really mean to walk by faith— day in and day out, in a world that constantly demands proof, explanations, and guarantees? Hebrews 11, known as the “Hall of Faith,” answers that question. This chapter is like a trophy room filled with men and women who trusted God when nothing around them made sense. Their stories still speak today, reminding us that faith is more than a belief in our hearts—it’s a lifestyle. WHAT FAITH IS Hebrews 11:1 says, “Now faith is the substance of things hoped for, the evidence of things not seen.” Faith is not wishful thinking. It’s not crossing your fingers and hoping things magically work out. "Faith is confidence in God before the result is visible." It means trusting that God is working, even when you can’t yet see the ending. 1. Substance means faith gives weight to what God promised. 2. Evidence means we act as if God’s Word is already true—because it is. Faith sees God’s faithfulness before the eyes ever see the miracle. WHY FAITH MATTERS TO GOD Hebrews 11:6 tells us plainly: “Without faith it is impossible to please God.” God isn’t impressed by our accomplishments, titles, money, or talents. He is pleased by our trust. To walk by faith means believing: 1. "God is who He says He is," and 2. "God will do what He said He will do." Faith honors God, and in return, God honors faith. FAITH IN ACTION: EXAMPLES FROM HEBREWS 11 The Bible doesn’t just define faith—it shows us what it looks like in real life. 1. Noah- obeyed when God told him to build an ark, even though rain like that had never been seen. "Faith obeys even when it doesn’t make sense." 2. Abraham- left his home not knowing where he was going. He simply followed God one step at a time. "Faith walks even without all the details" 3. Moses- chose God’s plan over Egypt’s wealth and comfort. "Faith values the eternal over the temporary." These heroes didn’t have perfect lives, and they didn’t have every answer. But they trusted God—and that trust changed history. WHAT WALKING BY FAITH LOOKS LIKE TODAY Walking by faith is not just a Bible story concept—it’s for us, right now. Walking by faith means: * When you don’t see the answer yet — you keep praying. * When the outcome is unclear — you still obey. * When fear whispers — you move forward anyway. * When life hurts — you keep trusting. Faith isn’t proven on the mountaintop—it’s proven in the valley. When everything in you wants to quit, faith says, “God is still able.” FAITH LOOKS BEYOND THE NOW Hebrews 11 tells us that many of those who trusted God “died in faith, not having received the promises.” (v. 13) Yet they kept believing because they saw something bigger—something eternal. Faith always looks beyond the moment, beyond the pain, and beyond this world. Why? Because our hope isn’t in what we see. Our hope is in the God who never fails. A FINAL WORD: KEEP WALKING Walking by faith isn’t a one-time decision. It’s a daily posture of the heart. There will be days when the path is dark, the answers are delayed, and the feelings don’t line up. But even then—especially then—God invites us to trust Him. When you can’t trace His hand, trust His heart. He has never failed, and He will not start with you. CALL TO ACTION Ask yourself today: Where is God asking me to trust Him more deeply? What step of faith is He calling me to take next? Make this your prayer: “Lord, I will walk by faith. I trust You, even when I don’t understand.”
